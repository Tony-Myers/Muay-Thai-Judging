Scorecards

Using the 10-point must scorecard in Muay Thai

Many events use a 10-point must scorecard. In this training approach, the card is a way to record who is ahead based on the Muay Thai criteria, not a requirement to score as a pure round-by-round strike count.

Practical meaning of common scores

ScoreTypical meaning
10–10Cannot pick a winner.
10–10+One boxer edges the round but does not win clearly.
10–9Clear round win.
10–8Knockdown (8-count), or total domination throughout the round.
10–7Two knockdowns (8-counts) in the round (possible variations depending on other action).

How to fill in the card reliably

  1. Indicate the fight number and confirm the corners are correct.
  2. Fill in every round.
  3. Indicate clearly who wins (the winner should have the higher total).
  4. Sign the card.
Reminder: clean kicks and knees to the body are often considered more effective than a cleanly landed punch of similar visible effect. Maintaining position and balance before, during and after delivery is central.
