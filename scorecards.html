Scorecards

Using the 10-point must scorecard in Muay Thai

Many events use a 10-point must scorecard. In this training approach, the card is a way to record who is ahead based on the Muay Thai criteria, not a requirement to score as a pure round-by-round strike count.

Practical meaning of common scores

ScoreTypical meaning
10–10Cannot pick a winner.
10–10+One boxer edges the round but does not win clearly.
10–9Clear round win (one boxer is clearly ahead on the criteria).
10–9Both boxers are knocked down, but one clearly wins the rest of the action in the round.
10–8Knockdown (8-count), or total domination throughout the round.
10–7Two knockdowns (8-counts) in the round (possible variations depending on other action).

Different point allocations are possible depending on what else happens in the round.

Worked examples: recording the developing lead

These examples show how the same round-by-round wins can lead to different totals depending on whether a boxer takes the overall lead and maintains it. Use 10–10+ (a small edge) when you cannot justify a clear 10–9 on the criteria.

Example 1: lead changes hands

RoundNoteScore
1Red slightly ahead10–10+
2Red stays ahead10–9
3Drawn round10–10
4Blue takes the lead9–10
5Blue maintains the lead9–10
Total48–49

Narrative: Red edged early, Blue swung the fight and maintained the lead.

Example 2: early lead holds

RoundNoteScore
1Red slightly ahead10–10+
2Red stays ahead10–9
3Red pulls ahead again10–9
4Red maintains the lead10–9
5Blue comes back, but not enough9–10
Total49–47

Narrative: Blue won the final round, but Red’s earlier lead remains decisive.

How to fill in the card reliably

  1. Indicate the fight number and confirm the corners are correct.
  2. Fill in every round.
  3. Indicate clearly who wins (the winner should have the higher total).
  4. Sign the card.
Reminder: clean kicks and knees to the body are often considered more effective than a cleanly landed punch of similar visible effect. Maintaining position and balance before, during and after delivery is central.
