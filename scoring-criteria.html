The Scoring Hierarchy

Judging is Top-Down. Judges look for the highest quality actions first. If fighters are equal on the top criteria, we move down the list.

1

Effect & Dominance

Visible impact (displacement, whiplash, redness). Actions that change the opponent's behavior.

2

Balance & Posture

Who remains composed? Who is disrupted? A fighter on the floor or stumbling is losing points.

3

Clean Scoring Weapons

Kicks & Knees: Score highly when clean and effective.
Punches: Score only if they show clear effect (snap-back, stun, knockdown).

4

Ring Generalship

Control of space, clinching dominance, turns, and dumps.

5

Aggressiveness (The 10%)

Forcing the action only scores if the fight is otherwise a draw. Pure aggression without effective landing scores nothing.

Common Misconceptions

What Scores

  • Body Kicks: Especially those that move the opponent.
  • Knees: Clean strikes to the body.
  • Teeps: That off-balance or disrupt rhythm.
  • Sweeps: Executed cleanly with the scorer remaining standing.

What Scores Little/Zero

  • Blocked Kicks: Hitting the arms/gloves without moving the fighter.
  • "Busy" Punches: Volume without visible impact.
  • Aggression: Moving forward while getting hit.
  • Holding: Clinching without kneeing or turning.
