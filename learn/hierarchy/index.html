Muay Thai scoring is a top-down hierarchy

Work from the top down. If the top tier is clear, lower tiers rarely overturn the decision. [oai_citation:5‡Guild of Muay Thai Officials judging presentation 2025.pdf](sediment://file_00000000054c71f4bdad69e3e27cbd60)

Direction does not matter.

A boxer can score moving forward, backward, sideways, or on the ropes. Judges reward effective scoring with balance and control, not simply advancing. [oai_citation:6‡Guild of Muay Thai Officials judging presentation 2025.pdf](sediment://file_00000000054c71f4bdad69e3e27cbd60)

Decision order (no percentages)

1) Effect and dominance

Visible impact or behavioural change: displacement, posture break, clear hurt, hesitation/avoidance.

2) Balance and posture

Who stays composed after scoring/receiving; who is turned, stumbled, or forced to reset.

3) Clean scoring weapons on scoring targets

Body kicks, knees, teep when clean and credible; punches and low kicks rise when they create clear effect.

4) Control of space and clinch

Ring command and clinch control that produces scoring (turns, posture breaks, clean knees, dumps).

5) Defence and counters that score

Defence that immediately leads to a clean, balanced scoring return (especially kick/knee/teep).

Strong vs weak signals

Strong signals (often decisive)

  • Displacement or posture break
  • Clear visible hurt or change in behaviour
  • Clean body kick/knee/teep with balance (not convincingly returned)

Weak signals (only matter when truly close)

  • High activity with little effect
  • Pressure that is neutralised or easily evaded
  • Contact clearly absorbed/checked with posture unaffected

This is why “landing more strikes” does not guarantee a win. [oai_citation:7‡Guild of Muay Thai Officials judging presentation 2025.pdf](sediment://file_00000000054c71f4bdad69e3e27cbd60)
