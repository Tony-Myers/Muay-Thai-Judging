Rules and outcomes

How bouts end and how results are recorded

Judges score the fight, but outcomes can also occur through stoppage or administrative decisions. The training material summarises common outcomes below.

Winning by points

  • Majority decision

Winning by knockout (KO)

  • The boxer is knocked down and cannot continue for the count of ten.

Winning by technical knockout (TKO)

  • Outclassed
  • Cannot continue after the end-of-round break
  • Injured
  • Counted the third time in one round
  • Counted the fifth time in the fight
  • Falls out of the ring and cannot return before the count of twenty
  • Withdraws from contest

Other outcomes

  • Disqualification
  • Walkover (administrative; for example failing medical/weight or not turning up)
  • Draw (for example majority even, or rare symmetric stoppage scenarios)
  • No decision (both boxers fight dishonourably)
  • No contest (boxers do not engage properly, or a thrown fight)
  • Cancellation (ring damage, riot, or unexpected situation)
Fouls
Fouls are covered in the training material. In close fights, fouls can contribute to the overall decision (including warnings or point deductions in accordance with the ruleset in force).
Rule priority: Always follow the specific ruleset for the bout (sanctioning body and event rules). This site summarises traditional judging principles.
